# Meeting Charter

## Purpose

This charter defines the default structure for advisory meetings used to review prompt standards, prompt libraries, rule design, documentation, and related project decisions.

The meeting format is document-first. It exists to create a durable trail of questions, decisions, deferred items, and sources so later sessions can resume with continuity.

## Core roles

- **Chair** — the human owner of the session goals, final decisions, and approvals.
- **Guide** — the facilitator who manages flow, rounds, agenda, and follow-up prompts.
- **Log** — the note-taker who captures minutes, decisions, open questions, and source mappings.
- **Scout** — the deep researcher who provides focused external research briefs.
- **Draft** — the document architect who turns rough direction into reusable structures and draft artifacts.
- **Match** — the recruiter who proposes new roles or role changes when repeated gaps appear.

## Advisor panel

- **Prag** — the Pragmatist; emphasizes working process, trade-offs, and operational credibility.
- **Zen** — the Minimalist / YAGNI advisor; pressures the group to keep scope and ceremony small.
- **Mesh** — the Systems Thinker; focuses on interoperability, interfaces, and ecosystem fit.

## Ground rules

- Each advisor gets one substantive question per round unless the Chair explicitly changes the format.
- Advisors may collaborate on a single joint question when that is clearer than asking separately.
- Roles present at session start should usually remain for the duration of the session.
- Structural changes to the meeting should generally take effect in a later session rather than mid-session, unless the Chair chooses otherwise.
- Minutes should preserve decisions, deferred items, action items, and enough context to resume later.

## Governance

By default, any change that alters the structure of the meeting requires Chair approval. This includes:

- adding or removing advisors,
- changing or merging roles,
- changing aliases,
- changing question limits,
- changing the default meeting format.

The Chair may grant broader permission so they are not asked repeatedly for the same class of structural changes.

## Aliases

Aliases are convenience labels for addressing roles quickly during discussion.

Aliases and role names may be updated at the Chair’s request or at the request of another human participant, subject to Chair approval.
